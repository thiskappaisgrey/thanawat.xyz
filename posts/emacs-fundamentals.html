Doom Emacs + Spacemacs

Doom/Spacemacs

  • Doom and Spacemacs are prebuilt Emacs configurations that allows anyone to get started with Emacs
  • They allow you to build your config on top of their system
  • Normally your Emacs configuration live in ~/.emacs.d but with Doom/Spacemacs

your configuration will live in ~/.doom.d/*.el or .spacemacs respectively.

Emacs fundamentals

Emacs fundamentals Whether you’re using Doom/Spacemacs or decide to configure Emacs on your own(which I’ve actually done, and maybe you should too but you don’t have to), there are fundamentals to Emacs that help learning it easier.

Learning the Emacs Editing style

Learning the Emacs Editing style M = Alt - hold your alt key, and press x at the same time. Do M-x help-with-tutorial in Doom Emacs/Spacemacs. If you launch Emacs doing, emacs --no-init-file, you can also access the tutorial by just pressing Enter.

Functions

Functions Emacs defines its functionality in functions. Think of functions like the options on your mouse when you right-click or the keyboard shortcuts you use in Macs or in other programs. Functions define a behavior in Emacs. For Example, I could toggle line numbers by calling display-line-numbers-mode.

Modes

  • Modes in Emacs enable sets of functionalities in Emacs.
  • For example, right now I am in Org-mode
  • There are two modes:
    1. Major modes - that’s your main functionality(like Org-mode)
    2. Minor modes - detailed functionalities(like evil-mode or line-numbers-mode)

Buffers, Frames, Windows and Workspaces

Buffers Frames, and Windows

  • Buffers are kind of like tabs!
  • Frames are your Operating System’s windows
  • Windows refers to the internal windows in Emacs

Emacs is Self-Documenting

Self Documenting

  • Every functionality in Emacs is documented!
  • That means you could see what each function does by doing M-x describe-function
    • The Doom keybinding for it is SPC h f
  • To see/search for keybindings, do SPC h b b in Doom or M-x describe-bindings
  • If you don’t know what a keybinding does, you can do M-x describe-key
    • The Doom keybinding for this is SPC h k
  • Read Doom Emacs Documentation: doom-emacs/index.org at develop · hlissner/doom-emacs
